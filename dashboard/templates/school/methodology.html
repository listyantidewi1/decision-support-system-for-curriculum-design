How It Works — Transparency & Explainability

This page explains how the curriculum recommendation system produces its results. We aim for full transparency so you can understand and trust the outputs.

Pipeline Overview

The system runs in two phases:

  • Phase 1: Extract skills and knowledge from job postings (JobBERT + LLM hybrid), map to future domains, detect trends, generate competencies, and export samples for human review.
  • Review: Experts review sampled skills, knowledge, and competencies. Feedback is stored per reviewer; majority vote merges multi-reviewer input.
  • Phase 2: Import feedback, apply corrections (Bloom, type), calibrate thresholds, re-verify skills, re-generate competencies, and regenerate plots. Final results reflect human input.

Extraction

Skills and knowledge are extracted using a hybrid approach:

  • JobBERT: BERT-based model fine-tuned on job postings; extracts skills/knowledge with confidence scores.
  • LLM: Large language model (e.g., GPT) for additional extraction.
  • Hybrid: When both agree, the item is marked BERT+GPT (typically more reliable). Single-model extractions (BERT-only, GPT-only) are also included.

The source column indicates which model(s) produced each item.

Scoring

Skill Priority Score

Curriculum recommendations combine three signals (coverage is for insights only, not prioritization):

  • Demand (40%): Job-market frequency — how many postings mention this skill.
  • Trend (30%): Empirical emerging/declining from time-series (FDR-controlled).
  • Future (30%): Forecast-aligned relevance from WEF/O*NET/McKinsey domains.
  • Coverage: Shown in plots for insights only. Existing curriculum may be outdated; schools use recommendations to design better curriculum.

Formula: priority_score = 0.40×demand + 0.30×trend + 0.30×future

Knowledge Score

Model score: 0.45×confidence + 0.25×future_weight + 0.30×occurrence

Competency Score

Based on related skills (max priority) plus human quality/relevance ratings (0.7×skill + 0.3×quality + relevance_adj + verify_adj).

Verification

Verification status reflects human review:

  • verified: Expert marked valid.
  • invalid: Expert marked invalid.
  • not_verified: Not yet reviewed. Not all items are in the review sample; unverified items may still be valid.

With multiple reviewers, majority vote is used. Cohen's Kappa for inter-rater reliability is shown when shared reviews exist.

Ranking Modes

  • model_only: Use pipeline scores only; human feedback shown but not used for ordering.
  • human_adjusted: Boost verified items (+0.05), penalize invalid (-0.15). Use when you have reviewed samples.

Trend Label (Why "Unknown"?)

Trend label shows whether a skill is Emerging (increasing), Declining (decreasing), or Stable over time, using FDR-controlled regression on job posting dates.

If most or all show "Unknown", common causes:

  • No job dates: Uploaded jobs must include a date column (e.g., posted_date, job_date). Preprocessing maps it to jobs_metadata.csv.
  • Insufficient time span: Need at least 3 distinct months of data for regression.
  • Low frequency: Skills with very few job mentions are excluded from trend analysis (min_jobs filter).

How to fix: Ensure your job upload CSV has a date column; run Phase 1 so enrich_with_dates and skill_time_trend_analysis can compute trends.

Future Weight

Skills and knowledge are mapped to forecast domains (WEF, O*NET, McKinsey) via SBERT embeddings. The similarity between an item and domain example terms is combined with the domain's growth trend. mapping_margin (top1 − top2 similarity) indicates confidence: large margin = confident mapping; small margin = ambiguous.

Limitations & Uncertainty

  • Sampling: Skills, knowledge, and competencies are stratified samples. Not all items are reviewed.
  • Unverified items: Items marked not_verified have not been reviewed; they may still be valid and appear in results.
  • Future mapping: Domain mapping uses embeddings; margin indicates confidence.
  • LLM: Competencies are LLM-generated; human review improves quality.
