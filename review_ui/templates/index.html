Review Rubric — when to choose Valid/Invalid, Quality 1–5, Relevant?

Skills & Knowledge — Valid?

Valid: Real skill/knowledge from the job text; actionable or meaningful concept. Invalid: Garbage, fragment, hallucination, not a skill/knowledge, or degree requirement. When unsure, choose Invalid.

Skills — Type & Bloom

Type: Hard = technical; Soft = interpersonal; Both = hybrid. Bloom: Remember, Understand, Apply, Analyze, Evaluate, Create, or N/A (soft skills).

Competency — Quality (1–5)

ScoreLabelMeaning
1PoorUnusable: incoherent, wrong grouping, misleading, or trivial
2FairWeak: unclear, partly wrong, or too narrow to be useful
3GoodAcceptable: clear, reasonable grouping, usable with minor edits
4StrongClear, well-structured, good level for curriculum
5ExcellentOutstanding: exemplary statement, integrative, curriculum-ready

Competency — Relevant?

Relevance = alignment with related skills + curriculum domain. Yes: Competency accurately groups skills and is useful for curriculum. Partial: Some fit, some don't. No: Misaligned with skills or not relevant to domain.

Competency — Skill focus

Hard: Technical, domain-specific. Soft: Interpersonal, behavioral. Both: Hybrid.
